THE CRIME

In 2018, a Supreme Court rescinded Dorothy Hung’s deed due to a technical notarization defect, but explicitly found her $245,200 payments were "undisputed." The money was never returned. By law, this created an equitable interest. No one may keep both the house and the purchase money.

THE LAW

Under New York law, keeping the money created a VENDEE’S LIEN (CPLR 3002(f)). This means they cannot evict her until every dollar is paid back.

THE TRAP

In 2020, attorneys pressured the elderly couple into signing a "stipulation"—a complex legal agreement to vacate the property—without getting a single dime of their savings back. In December 2025, the Trust returned to evict Mrs. Hung—now a widow.

THE PERJURY

In January 2026, despite knowing about the $245,000 lien, the Trust's attorneys told two separate courts that the money was just "rent" and that Mrs. Hung had no rights to the home.
